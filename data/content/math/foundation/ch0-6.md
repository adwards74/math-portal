# 0.6 Completing the Square
## Transform Any Quadratic into Vertex Form

            <div class="lesson-section">
                <div class="intuition-box glass" style="border-left-color: #00d2ff;">
                    <h3><i class="fas fa-magic"></i> The Core Idea</h3>
                    <p><strong>Completing the Square</strong> is a technique to rewrite $ax^2 + bx + c$ into the form $a(x-h)^2 + k$.</p>
                    <p>Why? Because from this form, you can instantly <strong>read off the vertex</strong> $(h, k)$ and <strong>solve for zeros easily</strong>.</p>
                    <p style="margin-top:10px; font-size:0.9rem; border-left:3px solid #00d2ff; padding-left:10px;">
                        This technique appears in: quadratic equations, calculus, physics, optimization problems. Master it here, use it everywhere!
                    </p>
                </div>

                <h2>I. The Perfect Square Trinomial Foundation</h2>
                <div class="proof-box glass" style="border:1px solid #00d2ff; padding:25px; margin-top:20px;">
                    <h4 style="color:#00d2ff;"><i class="fas fa-cube"></i> Key Pattern Recognition</h4>
                    <p style="margin:10px 0;">A <strong>perfect square trinomial</strong> factors as a perfect square:</p>
                    
                    <table style="width:100%; margin-top:15px;">
                        <tr style="background:rgba(0,210,255,0.1); border-bottom:1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.2);">
                            <th style="padding:10px;">trinomial</th>
                            <th style="padding:10px;">factored form</th>
                            <th style="padding:10px;">vertex form</th>
                        </tr>
                        <tr style="border-bottom:1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.1);">
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$x^2 + 6x + 9$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$(x+3)^2$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$(x-(-3))^2$, vertex at $(-3, 0)$</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr style="border-bottom:1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.1);">
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$x^2 - 8x + 16$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$(x-4)^2$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$(x-4)^2$, vertex at $(4, 0)$</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$x^2 + 10x + 25$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$(x+5)^2$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">vertex at $(-5, 0)$</td>
                        </tr>
                    </table>

                    <p style="margin-top:20px; background:rgba(0,210,255,0.1); padding:15px; border-radius:8px;">
                        <strong>🔑 The Pattern:</strong> If you have $x^2 + bx + ?$, the constant term must be $\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2$ to complete the square.<br><br>
                        <strong>Example:</strong> 
                        <ul>
                            <li>$x^2 + 6x + ?$ → Need: $\left(\frac{6}{2}\right)^2 = 3^2 = 9$ → $x^2 + 6x + 9 = (x+3)^2$</li>
                            <li>$x^2 - 8x + ?$ → Need: $\left(\frac{-8}{2}\right)^2 = (-4)^2 = 16$ → $x^2 - 8x + 16 = (x-4)^2$</li>
                        </ul>
                    </p>
                </div>

                <h2>II. Step-by-Step: Completing the Square</h2>
                
                <div class="example-box glass" style="margin-top:20px;">
                    <h4><i class="fas fa-list-ol"></i> Method for $y = x^2 + bx + c$</h4>
                    
                    <div style="background:rgba(0,210,255,0.1); padding:20px; border-radius:10px; margin:15px 0;">
                        <p style="margin:10px 0;"><strong>Step 1:</strong> Take the coefficient of $x$, divide by 2, square it.</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px; font-size:0.95rem;">This tells you what to add <strong>and subtract</strong>.</p>
                        
                        <p style="margin:15px 0;"><strong>Step 2:</strong> Add and subtract this value inside the equation.</p>
                        <p style="margin:15px 0;"><strong>Step 3:</strong> Group the first three terms (the perfect square trinomial) and simplify.</p>
                        <p style="margin:15px 0;"><strong>Step 4:</strong> Write in vertex form: $y = (x-h)^2 + k$.</p>
                    </div>

                    <h5 style="margin-top:20px; color:#00d2ff;">📝 Example 1: $y = x^2 + 6x + 5$</h5>
                    <div style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.15); padding:15px; border-radius:8px; margin:10px 0; border-left:4px solid #00d2ff;">
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 1:</strong> Coefficient of $x$ is 6. Divide by 2: $\frac{6}{2} = 3$. Square it: $3^2 = 9$.</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 2:</strong> $y = x^2 + 6x + 9 - 9 + 5$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 3:</strong> $y = (x^2 + 6x + 9) - 9 + 5 = (x+3)^2 - 4$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; font-weight:bold; color:#38ef7d;"><strong>Vertex Form:</strong> $y = (x+3)^2 - 4$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; font-size:0.9rem;">✓ Vertex: $(-3, -4)$ | Opens upward | Minimum value is $-4$</p>
                    </div>

                    <h5 style="margin-top:20px; color:#00d2ff;">📝 Example 2: $y = x^2 - 8x + 12$</h5>
                    <div style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.15); padding:15px; border-radius:8px; margin:10px 0; border-left:4px solid #00d2ff;">
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 1:</strong> Coefficient of $x$ is $-8$. Divide by 2: $\frac{-8}{2} = -4$. Square it: $(-4)^2 = 16$.</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 2:</strong> $y = x^2 - 8x + 16 - 16 + 12$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 3:</strong> $y = (x^2 - 8x + 16) - 16 + 12 = (x-4)^2 - 4$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; font-weight:bold; color:#38ef7d;"><strong>Vertex Form:</strong> $y = (x-4)^2 - 4$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; font-size:0.9rem;">✓ Vertex: $(4, -4)$ | Opens upward | Minimum value is $-4$</p>
                    </div>
                </div>

                <div class="example-box glass" style="margin-top:20px;">
                    <h4><i class="fas fa-list-ol"></i> With a Leading Coefficient: $y = ax^2 + bx + c$</h4>
                    <p style="margin:10px 0;">When $a \neq 1$, <strong>factor out $a$ first</strong>, then complete the square inside the parentheses.</p>

                    <h5 style="margin-top:15px; color:#00d2ff;">📝 Example 3: $y = 2x^2 + 12x + 10$</h5>
                    <div style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.15); padding:15px; border-radius:8px; margin:10px 0; border-left:4px solid #00d2ff;">
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 1:</strong> Factor out 2: $y = 2(x^2 + 6x) + 10$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 2:</strong> Inside the parentheses: coeff of $x$ is 6. Half: 3. Square: $3^2 = 9$.</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 3:</strong> $y = 2(x^2 + 6x + 9 - 9) + 10$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 4:</strong> $y = 2(x^2 + 6x + 9) - 2(9) + 10 = 2(x+3)^2 - 18 + 10$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; font-weight:bold; color:#38ef7d;"><strong>Vertex Form:</strong> $y = 2(x+3)^2 - 8$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; font-size:0.9rem;">✓ Vertex: $(-3, -8)$ | Opens upward (coefficient 2 > 0) | Minimum value is $-8$</p>
                    </div>

                    <h5 style="margin-top:20px; color:#00d2ff;">📝 Example 4: $y = -x^2 + 4x - 1$</h5>
                    <div style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.15); padding:15px; border-radius:8px; margin:10px 0; border-left:4px solid #00d2ff;">
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 1:</strong> Factor out $-1$: $y = -(x^2 - 4x) - 1$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 2:</strong> Inside: coeff of $x$ is $-4$. Half: $-2$. Square: $(-2)^2 = 4$.</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 3:</strong> $y = -(x^2 - 4x + 4 - 4) - 1$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 4:</strong> $y = -(x^2 - 4x + 4) - (-1)(4) - 1 = -(x-2)^2 + 4 - 1 = -(x-2)^2 + 3$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; font-weight:bold; color:#38ef7d;"><strong>Vertex Form:</strong> $y = -(x-2)^2 + 3$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; font-size:0.9rem;">✓ Vertex: $(2, 3)$ | Opens downward (coefficient $-1$ < 0) | Maximum value is $3$</p>
                    </div>
                </div>

                <h2>III. Solving Quadratic Equations by Completing the Square</h2>
                <div class="example-box glass" style="margin-top:20px;">
                    <h4><i class="fas fa-equals"></i> Finding Zeros</h4>
                    <p style="margin:10px 0;">Once you have vertex form $a(x-h)^2 + k = 0$, solving becomes straightforward!</p>

                    <h5 style="margin-top:15px; color:#00d2ff;">📝 Example 5: Solve $x^2 + 6x + 5 = 0$</h5>
                    <div style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.15); padding:15px; border-radius:8px; margin:10px 0; border-left:4px solid #00d2ff;">
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 1:</strong> Complete the square (from Example 1):</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; margin-left:20px;">$(x+3)^2 - 4 = 0$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 2:</strong> Isolate the squared term:</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; margin-left:20px;">$(x+3)^2 = 4$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 3:</strong> Take the square root of both sides:</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; margin-left:20px;">$x+3 = \pm 2$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 4:</strong> Solve for $x$:</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; margin-left:20px;">$x + 3 = 2 \quad \text{or} \quad x + 3 = -2$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; margin-left:20px;">$x = -1 \quad \text{or} \quad x = -5$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; font-weight:bold; color:#38ef7d;"><strong>Solutions:</strong> $x = -1, x = -5$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; font-size:0.9rem;">✓ Check: $(-1)^2 + 6(-1) + 5 = 1 - 6 + 5 = 0$ ✓</p>
                    </div>

                    <h5 style="margin-top:20px; color:#00d2ff;">📝 Example 6: Solve $x^2 - 4x + 1 = 0$</h5>
                    <div style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.15); padding:15px; border-radius:8px; margin:10px 0; border-left:4px solid #00d2ff;">
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 1:</strong> Complete the square:</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; margin-left:20px;">Half of $-4$ is $-2$. Square: $4$.</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; margin-left:20px;">$x^2 - 4x + 4 - 4 + 1 = 0$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; margin-left:20px;">$(x-2)^2 - 3 = 0$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 2:</strong> Isolate:</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; margin-left:20px;">$(x-2)^2 = 3$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 3:</strong> Square root:</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; margin-left:20px;">$x - 2 = \pm\sqrt{3}$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0;"><strong>Step 4:</strong> Solve:</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; margin-left:20px;">$x = 2 + \sqrt{3} \quad \text{or} \quad x = 2 - \sqrt{3}$</p>
                        <p style="margin:8px 0; font-weight:bold; color:#38ef7d;"><strong>Solutions:</strong> $x = 2 \pm \sqrt{3}$ (approximately $x \approx 3.73$ and $x \approx 0.27$)</p>
                    </div>
                </div>

                <h2>IV. Why This Derives the Quadratic Formula</h2>
                <div class="proof-box glass" style="border:1px solid #9d50bb; padding:20px; margin-top:20px;">
                    <h4 style="color:#9d50bb;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> The Big Picture</h4>
                    <p style="margin:10px 0;">The Quadratic Formula $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$ is <strong>literally</strong> the result of completing the square on $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$!</p>
                    <p style="margin:10px 0; font-size:0.9rem; background:rgba(157,80,187,0.1); padding:10px; border-radius:5px;">
                        This is why completing the square is so fundamental. It's not just a technique—it's the <strong>source</strong> of the formula itself.
                    </p>
                </div>

                <h2>V. Practice Problems</h2>
                <div class="practice-section glass" style="margin-top:20px; padding:20px;">

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="margin-bottom:25px; padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 1:</strong> Complete the square for $y = x^2 + 8x + 12$. Write in vertex form.</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">Half of 8 is 4. Square it: 16. So add and subtract 16.</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">$y = x^2 + 8x + 16 - 16 + 12 = (x+4)^2 - 4$<br>Vertex: $(-4, -4)$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="margin-bottom:25px; padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 2:</strong> Complete the square for $y = x^2 - 10x + 20$. Find the vertex.</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">Half of $-10$ is $-5$. Square: 25.</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">$y = (x-5)^2 - 5$. Vertex: $(5, -5)$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="margin-bottom:25px; padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 3:</strong> Complete the square for $y = 3x^2 + 12x + 9$. Write in vertex form.</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">First factor out 3. Then complete the square inside.</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">$y = 3(x^2 + 4x) + 9$<br>$= 3(x^2 + 4x + 4 - 4) + 9$<br>$= 3((x+2)^2 - 4) + 9 = 3(x+2)^2 - 12 + 9 = 3(x+2)^2 - 3$<br>Vertex: $(-2, -3)$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="margin-bottom:25px; padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 4:</strong> Solve $x^2 + 6x - 7 = 0$ by completing the square.</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">Complete the square: $(x+3)^2 - 9 - 7 = 0$, then $(x+3)^2 = 16$.</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">$(x+3)^2 - 16 = 0 \Rightarrow (x+3)^2 = 16$<br>$x + 3 = \pm 4$<br>$x = 1$ or $x = -7$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="margin-bottom:25px; padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 5:</strong> Solve $x^2 - 6x = -8$ by completing the square.</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">First move -8 to the left: $x^2 - 6x + 8 = 0$. Then complete the square.</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">$(x-3)^2 - 9 + 8 = 0 \Rightarrow (x-3)^2 = 1$<br>$x - 3 = \pm 1$<br>$x = 4$ or $x = 2$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 6 (Challenge):</strong> Solve $2x^2 + 8x + 3 = 0$ by completing the square. (Your answer will involve a radical.)</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">Factor out 2 first: $2(x^2 + 4x) + 3 = 0$. Then complete the square: $2((x+2)^2 - 4) + 3 = 0$.</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">$2(x+2)^2 - 8 + 3 = 0 \Rightarrow 2(x+2)^2 = 5 \Rightarrow (x+2)^2 = \frac{5}{2}$<br>$x + 2 = \pm\sqrt{\frac{5}{2}} = \pm\frac{\sqrt{10}}{2}$<br>$x = -2 \pm \frac{\sqrt{10}}{2}$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>
