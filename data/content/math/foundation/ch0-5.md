# 0.5 Imaginary Numbers & Complex Arithmetic
## Welcome to the Extended Number System

            <div class="lesson-section">
                <div class="intuition-box glass" style="border-left-color: #ff006e;">
                    <h3><i class="fas fa-sparkles"></i> The Problem That Started It All</h3>
                    <p>What is $\sqrt{-1}$?</p>
                    <p>For centuries, mathematicians said: "That doesn't exist." But in the 1500s, they realized: <strong>Let's just define it!</strong></p>
                    <p>We call $\sqrt{-1} = i$ (the "imaginary unit"). And suddenly, <strong>every quadratic equation has a solution</strong>.</p>
                </div>

                <h2>I. The Imaginary Unit</h2>
                <div class="proof-box glass" style="border:1px solid #ff006e; padding:25px; margin-top:20px;">
                    <h4 style="color:#ff006e;"><i class="fas fa-bolt"></i> The Key Definition</h4>
                    <p style="font-size:1.3rem; margin:15px 0;"><strong>$i^2 = -1$</strong></p>
                    <p>This is <strong>not a truth</strong> we derive. It's a <strong>definition</strong> we choose so that $\sqrt{-1}$ makes sense.</p>
                    <p style="margin-top:15px; font-size:0.95rem;"><strong>What about other powers of $i$?</strong></p>
                    <table style="width:100%; margin-top:10px;">
                        <tr style="border-bottom:1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.1);">
                            <td style="padding:8px;">$i^1 = i$</td>
                            <td style="padding:8px;">$i^5 = i \cdot i^4 = i \cdot 1 = i$</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr style="border-bottom:1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.1);">
                            <td style="padding:8px;">$i^2 = -1$</td>
                            <td style="padding:8px;">$i^6 = i^2 \cdot i^4 = -1 \cdot 1 = -1$</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr style="border-bottom:1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.1);">
                            <td style="padding:8px;">$i^3 = i \cdot i^2 = i \cdot (-1) = -i$</td>
                            <td style="padding:8px;">$i^7 = i^3 \cdot i^4 = -i \cdot 1 = -i$</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td style="padding:8px;">$i^4 = i^2 \cdot i^2 = (-1)(-1) = 1$</td>
                            <td style="padding:8px;">$i^8 = (i^4)^2 = 1^2 = 1$</td>
                        </tr>
                    </table>
                    <p style="margin-top:10px; background:rgba(255,0,110,0.1); padding:10px; border-radius:8px;">
                        🔄 <strong>Pattern Alert:</strong> Powers of $i$ repeat every 4: $i, -1, -i, 1, i, -1, -i, 1, ...$
                    </p>
                </div>

                <h2>II. Complex Numbers: Real + Imaginary</h2>
                <div class="example-box glass" style="margin-top:20px;">
                    <h4><i class="fas fa-sitemap"></i> The Grand Hierarchy</h4>
                    <p style="margin:15px 0;"><strong>Complex Number:</strong> Any number of the form $a + bi$ where $a, b$ are real.</p>
                    <ul style="margin:10px 0;">
                        <li>$a$ is the <strong>real part</strong></li>
                        <li>$b$ is the <strong>imaginary part</strong> (the coefficient of $i$)</li>
                    </ul>
                    
                    <table style="width:100%; margin-top:15px;">
                        <tr style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-bottom:1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.2);">
                            <th style="padding:10px; text-align:left;">Complex Number</th>
                            <th style="padding:10px; text-align:center;">Real Part ($a$)</th>
                            <th style="padding:10px; text-align:center;">Imaginary Part ($b$)</th>
                            <th style="padding:10px; text-align:left;">Type</th>
                        </tr>
                        <tr style="border-bottom:1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.1);">
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$3 + 2i$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px; text-align:center;">$3$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px; text-align:center;">$2$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">True Complex</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr style="border-bottom:1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.1);">
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$5 + 0i$ or just $5$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px; text-align:center;">$5$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px; text-align:center;">$0$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">Real Number</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr style="border-bottom:1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.1);">
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$0 + 7i$ or just $7i$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px; text-align:center;">$0$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px; text-align:center;">$7$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">Purely Imaginary</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">$2 - 5i$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px; text-align:center;">$2$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px; text-align:center;">$-5$</td>
                            <td style="padding:10px;">Complex (neg imaginary)</td>
                        </tr>
                    </table>
                </div>

                <h2>III. Operations with Complex Numbers</h2>
                
                <div class="example-box glass" style="margin-top:20px;">
                    <h4><i class="fas fa-plus-circle"></i> Addition & Subtraction</h4>
                    <p style="margin:10px 0;"><strong>Rule:</strong> Combine real parts together, imaginary parts together.</p>
                    
                    <div style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.15); padding:15px; border-radius:8px; margin:15px 0;">
                        <p><strong>Example 1:</strong> $(3 + 2i) + (5 + 4i)$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;">Real parts: $3 + 5 = 8$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;">Imaginary parts: $2i + 4i = 6i$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px; font-weight:bold; color:#38ef7d;">Answer: $8 + 6i$</p>
                    </div>

                    <div style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.15); padding:15px; border-radius:8px; margin:15px 0;">
                        <p><strong>Example 2:</strong> $(7 + 3i) - (2 - i)$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;">First, distribute the negative: $(7 + 3i) + (-2 + i)$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;">Real parts: $7 - 2 = 5$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;">Imaginary parts: $3i + i = 4i$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px; font-weight:bold; color:#38ef7d;">Answer: $5 + 4i$</p>
                    </div>
                </div>

                <div class="example-box glass" style="margin-top:20px;">
                    <h4><i class="fas fa-times-circle"></i> Multiplication</h4>
                    <p style="margin:10px 0;"><strong>Rule:</strong> Use FOIL (like normal algebra), then substitute $i^2 = -1$.</p>
                    
                    <div style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.15); padding:15px; border-radius:8px; margin:15px 0;">
                        <p><strong>Example 1:</strong> $(2 + 3i)(4 + i)$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;"><strong>FOIL:</strong></p>
                        <p style="margin:5px 0; margin-left:40px;">$F$: $2 \cdot 4 = 8$</p>
                        <p style="margin:5px 0; margin-left:40px;">$O$: $2 \cdot i = 2i$</p>
                        <p style="margin:5px 0; margin-left:40px;">$I$: $3i \cdot 4 = 12i$</p>
                        <p style="margin:5px 0; margin-left:40px;">$L$: $3i \cdot i = 3i^2 = 3(-1) = -3$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;">Combine: $8 + 2i + 12i - 3 = 5 + 14i$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px; font-weight:bold; color:#38ef7d;">Answer: $5 + 14i$</p>
                    </div>

                    <div style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.15); padding:15px; border-radius:8px; margin:15px 0;">
                        <p><strong>Example 2 (Special):</strong> $(3 + 2i)(3 - 2i)$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;"><strong>Pattern:</strong> $(a + bi)(a - bi) = a^2 + b^2$ (no imaginary part!)</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;">$= 3^2 + 2^2 = 9 + 4 = 13$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px; font-weight:bold; color:#38ef7d;">Answer: $13$ (purely real!)</p>
                    </div>
                </div>

                <div class="example-box glass" style="margin-top:20px;">
                    <h4><i class="fas fa-divide"></i> Division (Using Complex Conjugate)</h4>
                    <p style="margin:10px 0;"><strong>Rule:</strong> Multiply numerator and denominator by the <strong>conjugate</strong> of the denominator.</p>
                    <p style="margin:10px 0;"><strong>Conjugate:</strong> If you have $a + bi$, its conjugate is $a - bi$.</p>
                    
                    <div style="background:rgba(0,0,0,0.15); padding:15px; border-radius:8px; margin:15px 0;">
                        <p><strong>Example:</strong> $\frac{2 + 3i}{1 + i}$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;">Conjugate of denominator: $1 - i$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;">$= \frac{2 + 3i}{1 + i} \cdot \frac{1 - i}{1 - i}$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;"><strong>Numerator:</strong> $(2 + 3i)(1 - i) = 2 - 2i + 3i - 3i^2 = 2 + i - 3(-1) = 2 + i + 3 = 5 + i$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;"><strong>Denominator:</strong> $(1 + i)(1 - i) = 1 - i^2 = 1 - (-1) = 2$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px;">$= \frac{5 + i}{2}$</p>
                        <p style="margin:10px 0; margin-left:20px; font-weight:bold; color:#38ef7d;">Answer: $\frac{5}{2} + \frac{1}{2}i$</p>
                    </div>
                </div>

                <h2>IV. Why Imaginary Numbers Matter</h2>
                <div class="tj-edge glass" style="margin-top:20px;">
                    <h4><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Real-World Applications</h4>
                    <ul>
                        <li><strong>Electrical Engineering:</strong> AC voltage and current are modeled using complex numbers (called "phasors")</li>
                        <li><strong>Quantum Physics:</strong> Wave functions are complex-valued</li>
                        <li><strong>Control Systems:</strong> Stability analysis uses complex numbers</li>
                        <li><strong>Signal Processing:</strong> Fourier transforms work with complex numbers</li>
                    </ul>
                </div>

                <h2>V. Practice Problems</h2>
                <div class="practice-section glass" style="margin-top:20px; padding:20px;">

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="margin-bottom:25px; padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 1:</strong> Find $i^{23}$.</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">Powers of $i$ repeat every 4. Divide 23 by 4 and look at the remainder.</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">$23 = 4 \times 5 + 3$, so $i^{23} = i^3 = -i$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="margin-bottom:25px; padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 2:</strong> Compute $(4 + 5i) + (2 - 3i)$.</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">Add the real parts: $4 + 2$. Add the imaginary parts: $5i - 3i$.</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">Real: $4 + 2 = 6$. Imaginary: $5i - 3i = 2i$. Answer: $6 + 2i$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="margin-bottom:25px; padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 3:</strong> Multiply $(2 + i)(3 - 2i)$.</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">Use FOIL carefully. Remember $i \times (-2i) = -2i^2 = -2(-1) = 2$.</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">FOIL: $6 - 4i + 3i - 2i^2 = 6 - i - 2(-1) = 6 - i + 2 = 8 - i$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="margin-bottom:25px; padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 4:</strong> Find $(5 + 2i)(5 - 2i)$. (Hint: Look for a pattern!)</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">$(a + bi)(a - bi)$ always equals $a^2 + b^2$. What are $a$ and $b$ here?</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">$a = 5, b = 2$. So answer = $5^2 + 2^2 = 25 + 4 = 29$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="margin-bottom:25px; padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 5:</strong> Divide $\frac{3 + 4i}{2 - i}$. (Multiply by conjugate!)</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">Conjugate of $2 - i$ is $2 + i$. Multiply top and bottom by $2 + i$.</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">Numerator: $(3+4i)(2+i) = 6 + 3i + 8i + 4i^2 = 6 + 11i - 4 = 2 + 11i$<br>Denominator: $(2-i)(2+i) = 4 - i^2 = 4 - (-1) = 5$<br>Answer: $\frac{2 + 11i}{5} = \frac{2}{5} + \frac{11}{5}i$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>

                    <div class="practice-problem" style="padding:15px; background:rgba(0,0,0,0.2); border-radius:12px;">
                        <p><strong>Problem 6 (Challenge):</strong> Solve $x^2 + 2x + 5 = 0$ using the Quadratic Formula. Your answer will involve $i$!</p>
                        <details style="margin-top:10px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-cyan); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i> Hint</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px; font-size:0.9rem; color:#94a3b8;">$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$ with $a=1, b=2, c=5$. The discriminant $b^2 - 4ac = 4 - 20 = -16$. What is $\sqrt{-16}$?</p>
                        </details>
                        <details style="margin-top:8px;">
                            <summary style="color:var(--accent-green); cursor:pointer;"><i class="fas fa-check"></i> Solution</summary>
                            <p style="margin-top:8px;">$\sqrt{-16} = \sqrt{16} \cdot \sqrt{-1} = 4i$<br>$x = \frac{-2 \pm 4i}{2} = -1 \pm 2i$<br>The two solutions are $x = -1 + 2i$ and $x = -1 - 2i$</p>
                        </details>
                    </div>
                </div>
